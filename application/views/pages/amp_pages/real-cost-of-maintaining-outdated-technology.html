The Real Cost of Maintaining Outdated Technology

When it comes to maintaining legacy technology or software that suffers from functional gaps, the cost of doing nothing isn’t nothing. Failing to modernize technology eats into your margins at a time when efficiency is critical.

  • Technical Debt: According to Gartner, by 2025 companies will be spending 40% of their IT budgets on simply maintaining technical debt.
  • Security Risks: Security updates, vulnerability patches, and additional security layers could range between $100,000 to $500,000 annually, depending on the risks and industry regulations.
  • Lack of New Development: The Standish Group Chaos Report suggests that large organizations often spend over 90% of their IT budgets on maintaining bare bones systems, leaving only a small fraction for new development.
  • Bug Fixes and Patches: Companies spend around 15% to 25% of the original software development costs on bug fixes and patches for older systems.
  • Specialized Personnel: Salaries for specialized developers who can work on outdated technology are often 20% to 30% higher than salaries for resources dedicated to modern tech stacks.
  • Third-Party Support: If the software is dependent upon third-party vendors for continued support, annual fees can cost anywhere from $50,000 to $500,000 or more, depending on the system's size and complexity.
  • System Maintenance: A CIO survey from Deloitte reveals maintenance of sub-optimal systems consumes around 60% to 80% of IT budgets.
